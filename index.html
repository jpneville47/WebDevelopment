James Neville

About

I am a Mechanical Engineer with a specific focus in automated and electric applications in the transportation industry. In my undergraduate education at the University of Wisconsin - Madison I completed a Mechanical Engineering Major and a Computer Science Minor to give myself the appropriate foundation to merge mechanical and algorithmic systems. As the powertrain lead on UW's first electric Formula SAE vehicle, I had the opportunity to develop a custom gear box to independently apply power from the rear electric motors to the road surface. I have been employed at General Motors since 2017 with roles spanning artificial neural network applications in engine controls to innovation using design thinking. My current role is as a Software Integration Engineer for the mapping team on the UltraCruise program following 3 years as a System Capability Engineer on the same program. I am currently pursuing the Global Automotive and Manufacturing Engineering Masters degree at the University of Michigan.

Interest

The increasing maturity of autonomous and electric technologies has created new opportunities for automotive companies to reshape the way people interact with the world around them. Highly efficient electric powertrains have the potential to reduce the footprint that the transportation industry has on the environment. Autonomous vehicles can save lives and give people back the time they are currently losing while moving between locations, shifting how people perceive time and space. But these lofty goals are not without the potential of unforeseen consequences. Transitioning the entire transportation industry from internal combustion engines to electric motors could result in the strip mining of the constituent minerals from the ground resulting in unforeseen pollution. Autonomous vehicles, if offered to the public in states of partial fidelity, may result in consumers over trusting the feature so they are not ready to take control before a fatal incident occurs. It is important for the creators of these technologies to have an acute depth of technical understanding as well as an ability to work in global cross functional teams to produce the best outcomes for all stakeholders.
